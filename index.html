At the bustling terminal, an eccentric band of travelers awaited the arrival of their locomotive. To pass the time, Ye Old Man purchased a vintage wine, offering a heavy swig to Lil' Boh and The Beard. Seeking to ignite the spirits of the crowd, Master Bacon unfurled a set of bagpipes, filling the station with a roaring melody that sparked an impromptu dance party. Baron Von Halen swiftly cast *dancing lights* to illuminate the floor and laid down his lute, while Alice of Lore accompanied him on a flute. Not to be outdone, Ye Old Man conjured a brilliant disco ball above the travelers, and The Beard kept rhythm by juggling whistling throwing axes through the air.

Amidst the celebratory chaos, mischief took hold. Patti Goouya reached into the ether to cast *detect magic*, but her focus was shattered when Lil' Boh covertly slipped a glowing magical artifact straight from her person. The dance, however, proved lucrative, earning the light-footed performers a handsome sum of ten gold pieces from the amused onlookers.

As the train groaned to a halt at the platform, the mood shifted. Lil' Boh attempted to intimidate a station guard into surrendering his cheese, and Ye Old Man barked a similar demand; both failed miserably and were curtly ordered to board. Standing by the tracks, Elcava noticed an unusual presence: an excessive contingent of heavily armed guards keeping watch over a massive, reinforced box being loaded into one of the vault cars.

"Violence is fun!"
— Lil' Boh

Determined to ride in style, Lil' Boh tried to browbeat another guard into letting them into first class, but his thuggish threats fell short, and they were ushered into the standard cars with the rest of the cattle. Once aboard, Baron Von Halen retreated to the dining car, strumming his lute to busk for five quick gold pieces. Meanwhile, Ye Old Man began terrorizing the civilian passengers until an official guard intervened and scolded him for his foul behavior.

Near the rear of the train, Alice of Lore subtly conjured a *mage hand* to tamper with the locked door guarding the mysterious box and extra sentries, but the lock held firm. Back in the dining car, The Beard joined Baron Von Halen and bought a round of stiff drinks. The halfling bard critically failed his constitution check, succumbing instantly to the heavy spirits; his drunken fingers fumbled across his lute strings, and his music suffered terribly.

Desperate to clear out the passengers so they could crack the vault door, Patti Goouya conjured a localized cloud of skunk scent. The magic manifested as a horrific, invisible flatulence that choked the air. Gagging, the majority of the passengers fled the carriage in search of oxygen. Hoping to assist, Elcava blasted the vault lock with *ray of frost*. Unfortunately, freezing the mechanism solid rendered it entirely unpickable.

At that moment, The Beard entered the carriage, physically hauling the deeply intoxicated Baron Von Halen. Upon inhaling the terrible, lingering stench, The Beard vomited violently and collapsed onto the floor. Seeing the disaster, Ye Old Man cast *prestidigitation*, instantly scrubbing the vile odor from the air. Two guards rushed in to investigate the commotion. Alice of Lore quickly attempted to cast *charm person* on them both, but the guards possessed strong constitutions and shook off the enchantment entirely.

As Ye Old Man and Lil' Boh stepped outside to recover from the fumes, Master Bacon confronted the guards, loudly claiming the odor was an essential tenet of her holy religion and that their disgust was a theological insult. The guards were entirely unconvinced. The drunken Baron Von Halen tried to intervene with a slurred *vicious mockery*, but failed utterly. The Beard swings at them, giving the guards cause to promptly arrest him. The remaining guards ordered the party to open the windows, air out the car, and allow the returning passengers to take their assigned seats.

With the civilians filing back in, desperation mounted. Master Bacon took a roaring head start, leapt into the air, and drove her spear straight at the frozen lock at full speed—only to bounce off the door completely uninjured but thoroughly thwarted. Nearby, Ye Old Man was wandering around trying to purchase butter from confused travelers, none of whom understood his bizarre quest.

Ye Old Man: "Can I buy butter?"
Nate (DM): "How much?"
Ye Old Man: "Not a lot."

Seeking intelligence, Patti Goouya and Alice of Lore pooled their magical beast-speech abilities and dispatched Patti Goouya's companion bird to scout the train. When the avian spy returned, they learned a terrifying truth: the train's engine was fueled by an immensely powerful, volatile, and chaotic raw magical energy that would explode if damaged.

As the passengers finally drifted to sleep, a contingent of the party snuck forward toward the engine car. Ye Old Man dragged a reluctant Lil' Boh along, while Alice of Lore led a makeshift "traveling band of halflings" through the sleeping cars. Passing the winding-down dining car, Alice of Lore began playing a medieval rendition of Shakira. Baron Von Halen enthusiastically shook his hips in rhythm, while Ye Old Man tried to juggle his weapons, fumbling them loudly across the floor.

Reaching the engine door, they found it barred by two mechanical iron automata. Alice of Lore immediately tried to charm one of the machines, to no avail.

"I would like to cast Charm Person!"
— Alice of Lore (targeting a literal robot)

Baron Von Halen wracked his brain to remember any vital lore regarding this magical train, but the only absolute fact he could deduce was that they were, indubitably, riding on a train. Master Bacon attempted to forge an official clearance letter to trick the automated sentries, and Baron Von Halen cast *dancing lights* in a first-class cabin to draw the organic guards away, but the subversion failed. The party was firmly escorted back to their proper car by the guards. Defeated, Baron Von Halen failed an investigation check, The Beard failed to learn anything about the frozen door, and the party remained completely oblivious to their surroundings.

Refusing to give up, Master Bacon whipped out her thieves' tools and attacked the frozen lock again. Miraculously, her tools clicked through the pins successfully, but the frozen mechanism refused to budge.

Master Bacon: "Who’s a Wizard?"
Elcava: "I’m a Wizard!"
Master Bacon: "Girl, what are you doing?!"

Suddenly, disaster struck from above. A shadow eclipsed the moon as a massive flying pirate barge descended upon the train, and ten grizzled sky-pirates smashed through the glass!

The battle was chaotic fury. The Beard swung his greataxe into the fray, slicing a bandit, while Master Bacon hurled two daggers that missed entirely, forcing her to ready her body for a leap. Elcava unleashed two *magic missiles* that slammed into an assailant.

"Manscaped Pirates!"
— Lil' Boh

Alice of Lore cast *crown of madness*, snapping the mind of an elite pirate captain. Lil' Boh took two heavy cuts from enemy blades, entered a primal rage, and sheared a pirate completely in two with a single counter-swing. Baron Von Halen tried to cast *hideous laughter* but fumbled the spell. Ye Old Man swung a massive warhammer; he missed on his first swing, but his second blow delivered shattering damage before the weapon became stuck in the overhead luggage rack. Master Bacon, devoid of weapons, punched a wooden passenger chair with immense, aggressive energy. Elcava fired another *magic missile*, executing a nearby bandit on the spot.

Lil' Boh: "You get a gummy bear because you killed a bandit."
Ye Old Man: "Can I have a gummy bear because I’m Ye Old Man?"

The tide turned bloody. The crazed pirate captain under the crown's influence turned and delivered a venomous strike to Ye Old Man, while another pirate lunged and struck Elcava. Alice of Lore missed a desperate dagger thrust but buried her secondary blade deep into a pirate's side. The pirates retaliated against Alice but missed. Master Bacon landed a solid martial strike, but a pirate whipped around with a hand-crossbow, firing a bolt dipped in deadly toxin. The poison coursed through her veins, and Master Bacon collapsed, dead upon the floor.

Raging, Lil' Boh cleaved through the ranks, dealing devastating damage. Baron Von Halen tried to execute a dramatic power slide into the center of the horde to cast *thunderwave*, screaming "Hardcore Parkour!", but he wiped out completely and continued to strum his lute terribly. The Beard swung his greataxe and missed again. Elcava fired two *magic missiles* that went wide, while Lil' Boh and Alice of Lore were both struck by poisoned crossbow bolts. In a tragic turn, two stray bolts ricocheted across the metal car and thumped into Master Bacon's lifeless body.

Lil' Boh roared and cut yet another pirate cleanly in half. Baron Von Halen failed his *thunderwave* a second time, but accidentally killed a pirate by panicking and stabbing him squarely in the foot. Patti Goouya finally jolted awake from her slumber, looking around at the diminutive heroes.

"Are the pirates intimidated by you small people?"
— Patti Goouya

While Ye Old Man hurriedly cast a healing spell on himself, the pirates completed their true objective. In the absolute chaos, they smashed open the vault, secured the heavily guarded box, and began hoisting it up to their hovering airship via repelling ropes.

The Beard delivered a final strike to the maniacal captain. Elcava unleashed a desperate, wide-radius *thunderwave*. The concussive blast caught Ye Old Man and Lil' Boh, slamming them both into the broken wall of the train car. Ye Old Man was blasted clean out of the carriage, left hanging onto the side of the speeding train for dear life. The crazed pirate captain sliced Lil' Boh one final time before Alice of Lore stepped forward and finished the brute with her dagger. Lil' Boh leaned out the shattered wall and hauled Ye Old Man back to safety.

In a final act of desperation, Master Bacon's scouting bird flew out the window, attempting to claw at the pirates pulling up their ropes. But it was too late. The lines were severed, and the pirate barge ascended into the clouds, vanishing with the treasure. The train slowly pulled into the next station, leaving the battered, bewildered adventurers standing among the wreckage, thoroughly befuddled by the ride.
